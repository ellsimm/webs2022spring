Crying is actually good for you, but don't just take my word for it...

Is Crying Good for You? by Harvard Health Publishing

This article talks about the fact that we have all been crying a lot during these trying covid times. It puts tears into three categories including removing drebris and dust from our eyes, librucating our eyes, and finally (my personal favorite) emotional crying. The article suggests that the third is potenitally the most beneficial to our health! Crying as a response to emotional or physical pain actually releases toxins and produces endorphins, as opposed to the first two types of tears which reseachers found to be 99% water.

Another imporant point in here is the stigma surrounding this sort of expression of emotions, particularly in men and boys. Our society often sees crying in males as weak. The article points out the importance of letting boys know from an early age that crying and expressing emotions is not only ok, but also an important part of life.

Last super important piece - this article does mention that at a certain point, crying may be a sign of a problem. Excessive, uncontrollable tears definitely warrant a visit with a medical professional. See our resources page for a few links that may help you find someone to talk to.

Why You Should Cry by Penn Medicine

Accoring to Penn Medicine, there are five big benefits to letting it all out. Crying can...

  1. Improve your mood
  2. Improve your vision
  3. Protect your eyes
  4. Clean your nose
  5. Strengthen our relationships

"Nobody likes to cry, especially in front of others. Crying often times leaves individuals feeling vulnerable and at times even broken down or weak. However, when it comes to crying there is nothing to be ashamed of. In fact, crying offers many different health benefits that may cause some to argue that we should be crying more often" (pennmedicine.org).
