Resources

Sometimes you need more than just a good cry, and that's ok! Here are a few resources that can help

National Alliance on Mental Illness

"NAMI, the National Alliance on Mental Illness, is the nation's largest grassroots mental health organization dedicated to building better lives for the millions of Americans affected by mental illness" (NAMI.org).

University of Pittsburgh Counselling Center

Here you will find the Pitt Student Affairs website's Counseling Center webpage. You'll find the drop-in hours as well as opportunitites to make an appointment. Pitts offers various forms of group therapy ranging from tranditional discussion-based to a runner's group. This site is of course specific to Pitt students, but if you are a univesrsity student, definitely check out your school's options.

Turn2Me

"turn2me is a high-quality, safe, anonymous, and confidential space for you to gain support for your mental health online (turn2me.ie)."

This site is focused on online 1-on-1 conselling as well as facilitating virtual group therapy. Here you can find up to 6 free counselling sessions (for adults) as well as weekly, confidential support groups for a wide range of issues.

Therapist Aid

"Therapist Aid is dedicated to helping mental health professionals improve their craft by providing free evidence-based education and therapy tools. Our resources are created with clients in mind, which means avoiding jargon, and creating tools that are not only useful in theory, but in practice" (therapistaid.com).

So, you're proabably thinkings "I'm not a mental health professional," and that's ok! But, knowledge is power, and this site is full of it. It has tons of therapy worksheets, evidence-based research and techniques, articles, tools, and more. Dealing with your mental health can sometimes feel like a lonely and lost pursuit, but learning the science and reasoning behind whats happening can be more comforting than you think!
